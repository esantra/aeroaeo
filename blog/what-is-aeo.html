AEO BASICS

What Is AEO?

By Amanda Fouts, Founder of AeroAEO · Published June 24, 2026

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) is the practice of structuring and authenticating your content so that AI systems — like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews — can extract it, trust it, and cite it as a direct answer to a user's question.

The Shift That Made AEO Necessary

For two decades, the goal of online visibility was simple: rank in Google, get clicked. The game was about pages. The metric was position.

That game has changed. When someone asks ChatGPT "what's the best project management software for remote teams," they don't get ten links — they get a synthesized paragraph that names specific products and explains why. One of those names might be yours. Or it might not be.

AEO is the discipline of making sure it's yours.

What AEO Actually Involves

At its core, AEO is about making your content easy for machines to trust and extract. That means three things:

  • Structure: Using headings, lists, and schema markup so AI parsers can map your content to specific questions and answers.
  • Entity authority: Establishing a consistent, verifiable identity for your brand across the web — so AI systems can corroborate who you are and treat you as a credible source.
  • Answer density: Writing content that leads with direct answers rather than burying them in narrative. If the answer to a question is in paragraph seven, an AI model will likely find it somewhere else.

For a deeper look at the full framework — including the five pillars of AEO and a step-by-step optimization process — read The Definitive Guide to Answer Engine Optimization.

AEO vs. Traditional SEO

AEO and SEO share a foundation: you still need technically sound, authoritative content that clearly addresses real questions people are asking. But the finish line is different.

SEO success means ranking on page one and earning a click. AEO success means your content being the source an AI cites — often without any click at all. The user gets their answer, your brand name appears in that answer, and trust compounds over time.

If your content isn't optimized for extraction, you're invisible to an entire category of search behavior that's growing by the day.

Why It Matters Right Now

By late 2025, AI-powered answer engines had become the default starting point for a significant portion of commercial research queries. Buyers in the SaaS space, professional services, and high-consideration consumer categories routinely ask AI assistants for recommendations before they ever open a traditional search engine.

The brands showing up in those answers aren't there by accident. They've been optimized for it — or they got lucky once and haven't thought about it since. AEO is about making that presence intentional, consistent, and defensible.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does AEO stand for?

AEO stands for Answer Engine Optimization — the practice of structuring and authenticating content so AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews can extract and cite it as a direct answer.

Who needs AEO?

Any business that relies on being found online needs AEO — especially SaaS companies, professional services firms, and content-driven brands whose buyers use AI assistants to research options before making a decision. If your customers are asking AI "what should I use for X," you need to be in that answer.

How long does AEO take to work?

Most businesses see initial citation activity within 4–8 weeks of implementing structural and entity changes. Building lasting authority that gets cited consistently is an ongoing, months-long process — similar to the long-term compounding nature of SEO, but with faster early signals.
