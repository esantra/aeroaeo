AEO vs. SEO: Key Differences Every Marketer Needs to Know

By Amanda Fouts, Founder of AeroAEO · Published June 24, 2026

SEO and AEO both start with the same raw material — authoritative content that answers real questions. But they optimize for completely different outcomes, platforms, and definitions of success. Confusing the two will leave you missing half the visibility landscape.

They're Not the Same Discipline

I talk to marketers every week who assume AEO is just SEO with a new coat of paint. It isn't. The underlying goal is different, the platform is different, and the way "winning" is measured is fundamentally different.

That said, understanding what AEO is makes the comparison a lot cleaner — so if you haven't read that primer yet, start there.

Here's the direct side-by-side breakdown:

AEO vs. SEO at a Glance

Dimension SEO AEO
Goal Rank highly in search results so users click through to your page Be extracted and cited by AI systems as the direct answer to a query
Target platform Google, Bing, and traditional search engines ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, voice assistants
Content format Long-form pages optimized around keyword clusters, with internal linking and metadata Direct-answer structure with explicit Q&A formatting, schema markup, and clear entity attribution
Success metric Rankings, organic traffic, click-through rate Citation frequency, answer inclusion, brand mentions inside AI responses
Trust model Backlink authority — links from other sites act as votes of confidence Entity authority — consistent, verifiable identity across the web that AI systems can corroborate
Timeline to results Typically 3–6 months for meaningful ranking movement Initial citation signals often appear within 4–8 weeks; authority compounds over months

Where They Overlap

The overlap is real and important. Both disciplines reward:

  • Technical fundamentals: Fast, crawlable, mobile-friendly pages. If a bot can't access your content cleanly, neither SEO nor AEO will work.
  • Genuine expertise: Shallow, derivative content underperforms in both Google rankings and AI citation pools. There's no shortcut around depth.
  • Consistent publishing: Fresh, updated content signals active authority to both traditional search crawlers and AI retrieval systems.

The practical implication: a solid SEO foundation makes AEO implementation faster. If your site is technically clean and your content is substantive, you're already partway there.

The Key Tension: Clicks vs. Citations

The sharpest difference between SEO and AEO is what a "win" looks like. In SEO, you know you've won when traffic arrives at your page. In AEO, you've won when your brand is named inside an AI-generated answer — even if no one clicks through at all.

This feels counterintuitive to marketers trained on traffic metrics. But consider the buyer behavior: someone asks Perplexity "what AEO agency should I hire?" and the response mentions AeroAEO with a brief explanation of our approach. That buyer now has our name in their head before they've ever visited our site. That's brand presence at the top of the funnel, delivered inside the AI's own voice.

SEO doesn't do that. AEO does.

Do You Need Both?

Yes — and the answer isn't even close.

Traditional search engines still drive enormous traffic volume, and that won't flip overnight. SEO remains essential for capturing intent at every stage of the funnel, especially for transactional and navigational queries where users specifically want to visit a website.

But AI-powered answer engines are now the starting point for a growing share of research queries — particularly in B2B, SaaS, and high-consideration categories. If you're only doing SEO, you're invisible to those users at the very moment they're forming opinions about which brands to consider.

The businesses winning in 2026 are running both playbooks simultaneously. SEO builds the traffic floor; AEO builds the brand ceiling.
